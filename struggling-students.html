Critical Findings

The Core Problem: Doing the Work, Not Testing

These students average 135.5% of XP target—they're putting in the time. But only 29% have mastered their effective grade and 68% have test gaps of 21+ days. The issue isn't effort—it's testing cadence and pass rates. Students practice but don't validate learning through regular testing at 90%+ accuracy.

Focus Group: These 28 students are at grade 3+ level (passed 3.15 or have effective grade 3+) but grew less than 2x. They have the foundation but aren't progressing. This is the most actionable group—they have skills but need intervention on testing behavior.

The Core Insight

Students are practicing but not testing. 82% meet XP targets but only 29% have mastered their effective grade. The intervention needed is clear: increase testing frequency and ensure students pass tests at 90%+ before moving on. Practice without validation isn't producing growth.

Student Categories

Different patterns require different interventions.

18 students Doing Work But Not Mastering 64% of group

XP at 100%+ but haven't mastered effective grade. These students are putting in time but not translating practice to test performance. Long test gaps are common in this group.

Intervention: Mandate weekly testing. Review test-taking strategies. Consider whether practice content aligns with test content. Set specific test-passing goals with accountability.

11 students No Tests Passed at 90%+ 39% of group

These students have either: (a) taken tests but not achieved 90%+, (b) avoided testing entirely, or (c) are testing at wrong level. Need investigation to determine which.

Intervention: 1:1 check-in to understand testing barriers. Review test history to see if they're close to 90% or far off. May need content level adjustment or test prep support.

6 students Veterans (12+ months) Still Struggling 21% of group

Long-tenure students who should have progressed by now. Includes students with 41-65 months tenure. Long tenure without growth indicates systemic issues with their learning approach.

Intervention: Fresh start conversation. Review entire learning history. Consider complete reset of approach—what they've been doing isn't working. May need different modality or support structure.

5 students Below XP Target 18% of group

The minority who aren't meeting time/effort expectations. XP ranges from 21% to 89% of target. May indicate engagement, attendance, or motivation issues.

Intervention: Address engagement first. Can't expect growth without practice time. Identify barriers: attendance, motivation, technical issues, or competing priorities.

Recommended Intervention Program

Program Goal

Move 50%+ of these students (14+) to 2x growth by next assessment through targeted interventions on testing cadence, mastery validation, and closing the effort-to-outcome gap.

  • IMMEDIATE
    Weekly Testing Mandate for All 28 Students

    68% have test gaps of 21+ days. Implement mandatory weekly testing for all students in this group. Set up tracking dashboard. No student should go more than 7 days without attempting a grade-level test.

  • IMMEDIATE
    90%+ Mastery Focus for 11 Students

    11 students haven't passed any test at 90%+. Review their test history—are they close (80-89%) or far off? If close, focus on test prep. If far, may need content level adjustment. Don't let them keep practicing without achieving mastery.

  • IMMEDIATE
    Close-to-Mastery Push (8 students)

    8 students have highest passed within 1-2 grades of their effective grade (like Alex at 6.5 with effective 7). These students need focused push to pass their next grade level. Set 2-week sprint goals with daily check-ins.

  • SHORT-TERM
    Veteran Intervention (6 students)

    6 students with 12+ months tenure need fresh approach. Current methods aren't working. Consider: peer tutoring, different content track, goal-setting sessions, or environmental changes. Long struggle requires relationship-based intervention.

  • SHORT-TERM
    Low XP Engagement (5 students)

    5 students below XP target need engagement intervention first. Identify barriers: attendance, motivation, technical issues. Can't expect growth without practice time. Set daily XP goals with accountability.

  • ONGOING
    Test-Pass Celebration System

    Create visible recognition for passing tests at 90%+. These students are doing the work but need the dopamine hit of achievement. Celebrate test mastery, not just XP accumulation.

  • ONGOING
    Weekly Progress Review

    Track all 28 students weekly: tests attempted, tests passed at 90%+, test gaps, XP. Flag any student with 14+ day gap immediately. Don't let students drift back into non-testing patterns.
