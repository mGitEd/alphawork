Risk Pattern Analysis

Students often exhibit multiple overlapping risk patterns. Addressing these systematically can improve outcomes.

27 students Missing Key Apps (EGUMPP + Membean) 62.8% of struggling group

These students have zero XP in both EGUMPP and Membean—the two apps most strongly associated with high growth. This is the single largest addressable risk factor.

Intervention: Immediately onboard these students to EGUMPP (for language) and Membean (for vocabulary) with guided introduction sessions.

18 students Content Too Hard 41.9% of struggling group

Efficiency below 0.5 XP/min indicates content is too difficult. Students are spending time but not successfully completing work, leading to frustration and disengagement.

Intervention: Diagnostic assessment to determine appropriate grade level, then reset content difficulty. Consider temporary remediation track.

15 students Language-Heavy + Low Efficiency 34.9% of struggling group

These students spend disproportionate time on language (<35% vocab) AND have low language efficiency. They're stuck in a cycle of ineffective language practice.

Intervention: Rebalance time allocation to 45-50% vocabulary. Reduce language difficulty level. Add structured vocabulary instruction.

11 students High Time / Low XP (Disengagement) 25.6% of struggling group

Students with 2,500+ minutes but low XP output are likely disengaged—time on task without productive learning. May indicate gaming, distraction, or learned helplessness.

Intervention: 1:1 check-in to identify barriers. Consider engagement monitoring, shorter focused sessions, or different learning modalities.

10 students New Students (0-3 months) 23.3% of struggling group

20.8% of all new students are struggling—the highest rate of any tenure group. The onboarding period is critical and currently underperforming.

Intervention: Enhanced onboarding program with mentor pairing, clear expectations, and more frequent check-ins during first 90 days.

Recommended Intervention Program

Program Goal

Move 50%+ of struggling students (22+ students) to 2x growth or above within the next assessment period through targeted interventions addressing identified risk patterns.

  • IMMEDIATE
    Key App Onboarding Blitz

    Within 1 week: Identify all 27 students missing EGUMPP+Membean. Schedule guided onboarding sessions. Set up accounts with appropriate difficulty levels. Target: 100% of struggling students using both apps within 2 weeks.

  • IMMEDIATE
    Texas Sports Academy Emergency Protocol

    7 of 16 students (43.8%) struggling. Immediate site visit needed. Key issues: only 23% vocab time, 2/7 EGUMPP usage. Mandate minimum 40% vocab time allocation. Require EGUMPP for all eligible students.

  • IMMEDIATE
    Content Difficulty Reset

    For 18 students with <0.5 XP/min efficiency: Diagnostic assessment this week. Reset content to appropriate level (often 1-2 grades below current). Monitor for 2 weeks before re-evaluating.

  • SHORT-TERM
    Time Allocation Rebalancing

    For 19 language-heavy students: Implement minimum 40% vocabulary time requirement. Create structured vocab blocks. Reduce language time by shifting, not adding total time.

  • SHORT-TERM
    Level 2 Support Enhancement

    19.5% struggle rate in upper elementary. Add weekly small-group intervention sessions. Consider peer tutoring program with Middle School high performers. Review transition support from Level 1.

  • SHORT-TERM
    New Student Onboarding Overhaul

    20.8% of new students struggling. Implement 90-day intensive support: mentor pairing, weekly check-ins, clear milestone expectations, and early intervention triggers at 30/60/90 days.

  • ONGOING
    High-Time Student Monitoring

    11 students with 2,500+ minutes but low XP. Implement engagement monitoring dashboard. Flag students with time/XP ratio >3:1 for intervention. Consider session time limits with quality focus.

  • ONGOING
    Weekly Struggling Student Review

    Establish weekly review of all students below 1.5x growth. Track intervention compliance and effectiveness. Escalate non-responsive cases to specialist support.
