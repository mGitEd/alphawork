Critical Findings

The Core Problem: Engagement, Not Effort

Struggling students spend 19% more time than high performers (2,751 vs 2,307 minutes) but achieve dramatically lower growth. This isn't about working harder—it's about barriers to effective learning: content difficulty mismatch, disengagement, or challenges in the learning environment that more time alone won't solve.

Important Context: Students are expected to spend ~10 min on vocabulary and ~25 min on language daily (29%/71% split). EGUMPP and Membean are advanced apps that students progress into—lack of usage reflects their current level, not a missing intervention. Focus should be on actionable factors: engagement quality, content difficulty, and tenure-based patterns.

The Core Insight

Struggling students work longer but get less done. They spend 19% more time but produce 21% less XP with 29% lower language efficiency. The intervention needed isn't "more time"—it's addressing why time isn't translating to learning: content difficulty, engagement barriers, or environmental factors.

Actionable Risk Patterns

These patterns represent specific, addressable issues that can be targeted for intervention.

18 students Content Too Hard 41.9% of struggling group

XP efficiency below 0.5 XP/min indicates content is too difficult. Students are spending time but not successfully completing work, leading to frustration and disengagement. This is the most actionable pattern.

Intervention: Diagnostic assessment to determine appropriate level. Reset content difficulty (often 1-2 grades below current). Monitor efficiency for 2 weeks before re-evaluating.

11 students High Time / Low XP (Disengagement) 25.6% of struggling group

Students with 2,500+ minutes but low XP output are likely disengaged—time on task without productive learning. May indicate gaming, distraction, learned helplessness, or environmental barriers.

Intervention: 1:1 check-in to identify specific barriers. Consider engagement monitoring, shorter focused sessions, environmental assessment, or different learning modalities.

10 students Unknown Start Date 23.3% of struggling group

These students have no enrollment date on record, making tenure-based analysis impossible. Of all students with unknown tenure, 38.3% are struggling—the highest rate of any group. May indicate administrative gaps or special enrollment circumstances.

Intervention: First, investigate why enrollment data is missing—fix administrative tracking. Then assess each student individually to determine appropriate support based on actual time in program and current performance.

16 students Long-Tenure Veterans (2+ years) 37.2% of struggling group with known tenure

Students with 2+ years in the program have a 34% struggle rate—significantly higher than students new this year (24.8%). These veterans may have developed learned helplessness, disengagement, or need fundamentally different approaches.

Intervention: 1:1 conversation to understand barriers. Consider: fresh start with new content track, peer tutoring role reversal, goal-setting sessions, or environmental changes. Long-term struggle requires long-term relationship-based intervention.

9 students Low Language Efficiency Specifically 22% of struggling group with data

These students have <0.5 XP/min in language apps specifically, while vocab efficiency may be adequate. Language content is mismatched to their reading/comprehension level.

Intervention: Review MobyMax and Freckle placement. Consider reading level assessment. May need remediation track or additional scaffolding before current content level.

Recommended Intervention Program

Program Goal

Move 50%+ of struggling students (22+ students) to 2x growth or above within the next assessment period through targeted interventions addressing identified barriers: content difficulty, engagement issues, new student support, and site-specific problems.

  • IMMEDIATE
    Content Difficulty Reset for 18 Students

    Students with <0.5 XP/min efficiency need diagnostic assessment THIS WEEK. Reset content to appropriate level—typically 1-2 grades below current placement. This is the most direct path to improved outcomes.

  • IMMEDIATE
    Texas Sports Academy Site Review

    7 of 16 students (43.8%) struggling is a systemic issue. Schedule immediate site visit. Review: learning environment, guide support, technical issues, and any site-specific barriers. This isn't 7 individual problems—it's one site problem.

  • IMMEDIATE
    Unknown Tenure Data Investigation (10 students)

    These 10 students have no enrollment date on record AND are struggling (38.3% rate—highest of any group). Fix the administrative tracking gap, then assess each student individually. Data quality issues may indicate broader tracking problems.

  • IMMEDIATE
    Veteran Student Intervention (16 students)

    Students with 2+ years tenure have a 34% struggle rate—the paradox of long tenure without improvement. These students need 1:1 conversations to understand barriers, fresh approaches, and possibly a complete reset of their learning track. More of the same won't work.

  • SHORT-TERM
    High-Time/Low-Output Investigation (11 students)

    1:1 check-ins for students with 2,500+ min but low XP. Identify specific barriers: Is it distraction? Gaming? Environmental issues? Learned helplessness? Each may need different intervention. Consider shorter, focused sessions.

  • SHORT-TERM
    Level 2 (4th-5th Grade) Support Enhancement

    19.5% struggle rate at Level 2—highest of any level. Review transition support from Level 1. Consider small-group intervention sessions, peer tutoring, or additional scaffolding for complex content.

  • ONGOING
    Veteran Support Program

    Create specialized intervention track for students with 2+ years who are still struggling. New onboarding appears to be working (24.8% struggle rate)—apply those learnings to veteran re-engagement. Consider: fresh content tracks, peer mentoring opportunities, goal-setting workshops.

  • ONGOING
    Weekly Struggling Student Review

    Establish weekly review of all students below 1.5x growth. Track: intervention compliance, efficiency trends, engagement metrics. Escalate non-responsive cases. Don't let students drift—active monitoring catches issues early.
